## Full description (Steam BBCode)

[h1]A mod where you sell dru.. [b]*ahem!*[/b] You're selling RUGS![/h1]

RUGS! adds a street-trading layer to Big Ambitions. Dealers stand on corners across Manhattan, working a market that moves daily and differs block to block. You buy where it's cheap, haul it across town, and sell where it isn't. The money you make is dirty, the IRS is the only law in this city, and laundering is how you keep what you earn.

The mod stays dormant until your first deal. Never deal with a dealer and your save plays as pure vanilla.

[h2]The product line — 9 rugs[/h2]
[list]
[*][b]Common, sold all over town:[/b] REED, ROCAINE, RETH, ROPE, RANEX
[*][b]Rare, never on your home block:[/b] RUSHROOMS, RADDERALL, REROIN, RENTANYL
[/list]

[h2]Features[/h2]
[list]
[*][b]22 hand-placed dealers across 6 districts.[/b] Find them and learn their corners.
[*][b]A market that moves.[/b] Every district rolls its own price on every rug, every day — the same rug can be cheap on one block and triple across town. Buy price equals sell price; the profit is in the hauling.
[*][b]The Plug.[/b] A phone contact who texts a daily wire — going rates on every rug, your books, your heat, the occasional tip.
[*][b]Dirty money and heat.[/b] Rug cash is unexplained income. Sit on too much, or move the nastier product, and the IRS audits you. No police in this game — the taxman is worse.
[*][b]Real cargo.[/b] Rugs are carried, stacked, hauled on a hand truck, and stashed at your businesses like any other goods. They're just worthless to everyone except a dealer.
[*][b]Laundering.[/b] Wash dirty cash through a business you own — it clears overnight as booked, taxed revenue — or take an instant quick-wash for a cut. One button washes the whole stash across every front at once. Wash more than the books can explain and the audit finds you.
[*][b]CROOKBOOKS™ by RUGSOFT.[/b] Any computer at home boots the operation's own accounting software, vintage 1989 — boot screen, main menu, CRT glass and all. Two programs on the disk: THE BOOKS and THE WASH. (please give us a laundry machine!)
[*][b]THE BOOKS.[/b] The whole operation on one green screen — every business, its role, its dirty take, its wash room, and the levers to switch them. Collect every rider's cash in one click. Washing goes automatic once you run five businesses.
[*][b]Factories feed the machine.[/b] Supply your own stores from your own factory and they wash and earn bigger. The deeper the legit empire, the harder the dirty one runs.
[*][b]Street events.[/b] Walking up to a dealer can trigger something first — a found stash, a robbery, a shakedown, a night that ends at the hospital. At most one per neighborhood per day.
[*][b]Your home corner.[/b] The dealer nearest your place buys every rug, common or rare — the guaranteed spot to dump a whole haul.
[/list]

[h2]How to play[/h2]
[olist]
[*]Find a dealer on a corner and talk to him.
[*]Buy low. Rugs ride in a box in your hands.
[*]Read the Plug's wire, cross town, sell where the price is right.
[*]Wash the cash from a computer at home — through your own business (cheap, limited by its books) or instant for a cut.
[*]Watch your heat. The IRS is patient, not blind.
[/olist]

[h2]What's new — 1.6.0.4.2.0[/h2]
[list]
[*][b]CROOKBOOKS™ by RUGSOFT.[/b] The laundry computer got a full overhaul into one period-correct app. It boots (watch the POST — 640K RAM ... OK, TWO SETS OF BOOKS ... FOUND), drops you at a main menu, and runs on number keys and Esc like it's 1989. Any key skips the boot.
[*][b]THE BOOKS.[/b] The Grand Ledger, renamed and moved in: businesses, roles, intake, wash room, riders, night crew — plus [b]COLLECT ALL[/b], which sweeps every rider's held cash in one click.
[*][b]THE WASH.[/b] The laundering screen, now in the same green: quick wash, per-front washing, and [b]WASH ALL[/b] — one click runs the night crew's safe sweep by hand, biggest room first, no cut, any time.
[*][b]The glass.[/b] Scanlines, phosphor vignette, a whisper of flicker, command prompts, key legends. Everything still clicks the same underneath.
[*][b]Washed-out rows tell you why.[/b] A front that's done for the day now says what moved through it ("washed $57,132 today") instead of a wall of "tapped out" that read like a bug.
